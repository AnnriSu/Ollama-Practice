

 COLOGNE CATHEDRAL (KÖLNER DOM) 

 I. OVERVIEW & IDENTIFICATION
 Official Name: Cologne Cathedral (Kölner Dom)
 Location: Cologne, Germany
 Style: Gothic (High Gothic / Gothic Revival)
 Status: UNESCO World Heritage Site
 Key Distinction: Germany’s most visited landmark
 Construction Dates: Began 1248, Completed 1880 (632 years total)

 II. ARCHITECTURAL & STRUCTURAL DATA
 Height (Spires): 157 meters
 Length: 144.6 meters
 Nave Height: ~45 meters
 Structure: 8-bay nave, 4-bay choir, double aisles, Chevet with ambulatory and 7 chapels
 Inspiration: Amiens Cathedral (French Gothic)
 Façade: West façade based on 4-meter Medieval drawing

 III. HISTORICAL TIMELINE
 1164: Relics of the Three Wise Men arrive.
 1248: Construction starts (Archbishop Konrad von Hochstaden, architect Meister Gerhard).
 1322: Choir completed and consecrated.
 16th Century - 1842: Construction halted (300+ years).
 1842: Construction resumes (architect Ernst Friedrich Zwirner).
 1880: Completion.
 WWII: Struck 14 times; used as Allied navigation reference.
 1956: Interior fully repaired post-war.

 IV. KEY FEATURES & TREASURES
 Main Treasure: Shrine of the Three Kings (crafted by Nicholas of Verdun).
 Famous Artwork 1: Gero Crucifix (10th century, oldest life-sized wooden crucifix).
 Interior Furniture: Choir Stalls (1308–1311, 104 seats).
 Stained Glass 1: "Bible Window" (c. 1261, oldest biblical window in Germany).
 Treasury: Germany's richest (located in north transept; contains Crosier of St. Peter, etc.).
