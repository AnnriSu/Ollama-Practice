COLOGNE CATHEDRAL (KÖLNER DOM)

I. Overview & Identification
• Official Name: Cologne Cathedral (Kölner Dom)
• Location: Cologne, Germany (directly beside Köln Hauptbahnhof / Main Railway Station)
• Style: Gothic (High Gothic / Gothic Revival)
• Status: UNESCO World Heritage Site
• Key Distinction: Germany’s most visited landmark
• Construction Dates: Began 1248, Completed 1880 (632 years total)

II. Architectural & Structural Data
• General Style
  • High Gothic architecture with detailed, symmetrical, vertical design
  • Length: 144.6m; Nave height: 45m
• Structure
  • Eight-bay nave, four-bay choir, both with double aisles
  • Larger, deeper west towers define the silhouette
  • Eastern chevet with ambulatory + seven chapels
  • Quadripartite rib vaults
  • Inspired by Amiens Cathedral—slimmer supports, 3:1:3 elevation with aligned triforium and clerestory
• Façades
  • West façade based on a medieval 4-meter Gothic drawing
  • Square towers transitioning to octagonal upper levels with openwork spires
  • Transept façades redesigned in the 19th century (Gothic Revival by Ernst Friedrich Zwirner)
• Interior Features
  • Sculptures of Christ, Virgin Mary, and Twelve Apostles
  • Gero Crucifix (10th century), oldest life-sized wooden crucifix
  • Oak choir stalls (1308–1311) with 104 seats
  • High altar shows influences from Lorraine, Cologne, and Paris
  • Cathedral workshop later developed its own Gothic style
• Stained Glass Windows
  • Medieval windows including the “Bible Window” (c. 1261), oldest biblical window in Germany
  • 18th-century replacement of colored glass with white panes
  • 19th-century restoration using glass from other Cologne churches
  • Notable artists: Master of St. Veronica, Bartholomäus Bruyn the Elder
  • Themes: History of Salvation, Three Kings
• Treasury
  • Largest and richest cathedral treasury in Germany
  • Holds relics, chalices, monstrances, and liturgical objects—many still used
  • Highlights: Crosier of St. Peter, 12th-century Shrine of the Cross, c.1400 Gothic monstrance, Shrine of the Three Kings, illuminated manuscripts (e.g., Limburg Gospels)


III. Historical Timeline
• 1164: Relics of the Three Wise Men arrive
• 1248: Construction starts (Archbishop Konrad von Hochstaden, architect Meister Gerhard)
• 1322: Choir completed and consecrated
• 16th Century – 1842: Construction halted (300+ years)
• 1842: Construction resumes (architect Ernst Friedrich Zwirner)
• 1880: Completion
• WWII: Struck 14 times; used as Allied navigation reference
• 1956: Interior fully repaired post-war

IV. Key Features & Treasures
• Main Treasure: Shrine of the Three Kings (crafted by Nicholas of Verdun)
• Famous Artwork: Gero Crucifix (10th century, oldest life-sized wooden crucifix)
• Interior Furniture: Choir Stalls (1308–1311, 104 seats)
• Stained Glass: "Bible Window" (c. 1261, oldest biblical window in Germany)
• Treasury: Germany's richest (north transept; contains Crosier of St. Peter, etc.)

VI. Best Time & Transport
• Ideal Visit Time: Weekday mornings, 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM (low crowds)
• Time to Avoid: 11:00 AM – 3:00 PM
• Best Seasons: Autumn (Sept–Oct) or Spring (Apr–May)
• Crowd Events: Carnival (February), Christmas Market (December)
• Getting There
  • Cologne is well connected by national transportation networks.
  • The city has its own international airport.
  • Cologne Cathedral sits directly beside Köln Hauptbahnhof (main train station).
  • Its tall spires make it easy to spot from many parts of the city.
